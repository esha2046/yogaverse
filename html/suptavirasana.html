FOR ADVANCED

SUPTAVIRASANA

  • Start in an upright kneeling position with both shins and the tops of the feet on the earth The hips
    are over the knees.
  • Bring the knees together as you separate the feet to just enough space where you can sit your
    buttocks between them.
  • On an exhale gently sit the butt down to the floor or a block. You can manually move your
    calf muscles outwards to create a little more space.
  • Once you are settled in this pose, bring the hands to the earth behind you. Shift the weight into
    the hands and then bring the elbows and forearms to the earth.
  • Rest your arms alongside your body or grasp opposite elbows above head.
  • Lift your sternum, lengthen the spine, and broaden the collarbones.

BENEFITS

  • Supta Virasana stretches the abdominal and thigh muscles.
  • This pose can strengthen the hip region and brings flexibility to the back, but those suffering from sacral pain should consult a doctor before attempting this.
  • It brings flexibility to the lower back and hips.
  • For women, it improves health of reproductive organs.
